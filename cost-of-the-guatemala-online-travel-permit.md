# Cost of the Guatemala Online Travel Permit

Travelers entering Guatemala are required to complete an online immigration declaration before arrival or departure. This digital document, commonly called the **Regional Traveler Affidavit (Declaración Jurada Regional de Viajero – DJRV)**, replaces the old paper declaration forms that were previously handed out on airplanes or completed at airports. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

Understanding the cost of this online travel permit can be confusing because official government forms are free, while some private websites charge a fee based on additoinal services like customer support among others.

**This guide explains the real cost of the Guatemala online travel permit, how the system works, and what travelers should expect before their trip.**

---

## Is the Guatemala Online Travel Permit Free?

In most cases, the official **[Guatemala online immigration declaration](https://guatemalaentryform.com/) form is free when completed on the government website**. Travelers can submit the form online before their trip and receive a confirmation QR code that must be presented at immigration. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

The digital system was introduced to modernize the entry process and reduce paperwork at airports. Instead of filling out paper forms during a flight or upon arrival, travelers now submit their information electronically before reaching Guatemala. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

Because the form is handled online, the process is generally faster and helps immigration officers process passengers more efficiently when they arrive in the country.

---

## Why Some Websites Charge a Fee

While the official immigration declaration itself is free, many private visa-assistance websites offer help with completing and submitting the form. These services typically charge a **processing or service fee**, even though the underlying document does not require payment when submitted directly through the government portal. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

Typical third-party service fees can include:

- Application processing assistance  
- Customer support during the submission process  
- Faster document preparation or delivery  
- Email delivery of the QR code confirmation  

Some private websites charge around **$70–$78 USD per applicant** for assistance with the application. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

It is important for travelers to understand that these charges are optional and are not required by the Guatemalan government. Applying directly through the official system allows travelers to avoid these additional costs.

---

## What the Online Travel Permit Covers

The Guatemala online declaration form is not a visa or entry permit in the traditional sense. Instead, it is a **customs and immigration declaration document** that allows travelers to provide required information before entering or leaving the country. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  

The form is used to declare:

- Personal travel information and passport details  
- Flight and travel itinerary  
- Goods or merchandise entering the country  
- Cash amounts exceeding USD 10,000  
- Certain regulated items such as plants, animals, or food products  

This declaration helps authorities monitor customs regulations and ensure that restricted or taxable items are properly declared when travelers cross the border. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}  

---

## When You Need to Complete the Form

Travelers must submit the immigration declaration **before entering or leaving Guatemala**. It is typically completed online within the days leading up to a trip, and the confirmation is sent by email in the form of a QR code. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}  

After completing the form, travelers should either print the confirmation or save a screenshot of the QR code on their phone. Immigration or customs officers may request it when entering or exiting the country. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}  

The form applies to most travelers entering Guatemala by air and helps speed up the processing of passengers at airports.

---

## Additional Travel Costs to Know About

Although the immigration declaration form itself is usually free, travelers should be aware of other potential travel-related fees when visiting Guatemala.

Common additional costs include:

- Airport departure tax (usually about **$30 USD**, often included in airline tickets)  
- Visa fees if required for certain nationalities  
- Optional third-party processing services for immigration forms  

These costs are separate from the digital travel declaration but may appear during the overall travel process.

---

## In Brief

The Guatemala online travel permit—formally known as the **Regional Traveler Affidavit (DJRV)**—is a digital immigration declaration required for travelers entering or leaving the country. The official form can usually be completed **free of charge through the government website**, and it generates a QR code used during immigration processing.  

However, some private websites charge service fees to assist travelers with the application process. While these services can be convenient, they are optional and not required to enter Guatemala.  

By understanding the difference between the official free declaration and paid third-party services, travelers can avoid unnecessary costs and complete the process quickly before their trip.
